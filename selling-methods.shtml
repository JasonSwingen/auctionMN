Fees And General Information About Each Type Of Auction

1. Live auction at your site or a rented site

These fees vary depending on the amount of time necessary to prepare and conduct your auction and the anticipated proceeds of the sale. Our fees are very competitive and usually average around 25%. There are also additional fees for set-up and advertising. Much smaller fees are charged for high value items such as cars and the like.

2. Live Tuesday auctions at our site which is Mau’s Corner near St. Francis from April through September

We hold auctions every Tuesday at Mau's Corner from April through September that begin at 9 a.m. Mau's Corner is 3 miles north of St. Francis on Highway 47. The auction is outdoors, so please check our website for information about weather cancellations.

If you bring things to Mau’s Corner for our Tuesday auctions, we recommend that you arrive by 6 a.m. Generally, we sell items in the order they are brought to us in the morning. However, those sellers who sell $250 or more at the preceding auction are allowed to set out their items ahead of most everyone else.

Sellers set out their own items for easy inspection in the areas we designate. You can bring your own tables or borrow tables from us so long as we have some. If you borrow our tables, you will need to bring them back to the stack when you are done using them. There is a $2.00 per table fee if we have to retrieve them and bring them back to the stack for you. This may be assessed at a subsequent auction. Items may also be set on the ground or in some cases left on a trailer.

Please be prepared to unload or load your items yourself. AAA Auction does not provide any manpower to load or unload any items.

You must remove any of your items that do not sell right after the auction. Any unsold items left at the auction site when the auction ends will be disposed by AAA Auction. Disposal fees may apply.

If you are present at the end of the auction, and if your selling area is all picked-up, we will pay you for your sold items on the spot. If you are not present when the auction is over, we will mail your check to you in about one week.

We don't accept any bids under $5.00 and we group items together if necessary to try to get at least a $5 bid. If we cannot get a bid of at least $5.00 for your item or group of items, we charge at $2.00 No-Sale fee. The fees below are based upon you bringing your items to the live auction. Higher fees are charged if we pick up your items.

The fees are per item as follows:

Sale Price Fee
$1 to $599 10%
$600 and up Free
Firearms Free

Sellers shall also pay a $5 processing fee on every lot offered for sale.

3. Online estate auction at your site

At a traditional estate sale, the operator prices all of your items and the public comes into your home and shops. At an online estate auction, the prices are determined by competitive bidding that bidders do from their home and not in your house. We will come to your home and photograph all of the items to be sold. We will then type descriptions of those items and hold the online estate auction for about 9 days. The winners will then come to your home to pick up their items. We will handle all for this for you. Our fees for online estate auctions vary depending on the amount of work necessary to prepare and sell your items and the anticipated proceeds of the sale. Our fees are very competitive and usually 20% to 40%.

Here are some good reasons to use the online estate auction method:

  • Online estate auctions avoid having a person price something so it does not sell or so low it sells right away for less money than it is worth.
  • Competitive bidding means that you get top dollar on each item you sell.
  • There is seldom anything left over after an auction.
  • You don't have hordes of people wandering through your house.
  • More people are exposed to your items in our online estate auctions. We have around 40,000 page hits for each online auction we do.
  • We pay our clients very quickly (in about 7-10 days), not in six weeks like most estate sale companies.
  • We describe each item and often include several photographs of each item as well. Most estate sale companies take only one photograph of an item and do not describe it very well.
  • When required, we have certified appraisers on our staff who can provide you with well-researched opinions on the value of an item.

5. Outright sale to AAA Auction of your items

One of our professional appraisers will review your items and make you an offer. Payment is made in cash and the pick-up of your items can be done quickly. There are a couple of ways to do this.

  • A Pick - When we do a pick, we determine one overall price that we offer to you. We will then take whatever we choose from the items you have to sell.
  • Clean-Out - Some sellers need everything removed from their property. We can do that too. We will pay you the seller for the items if there is adequate value in selling the items to cover our clean-out overhead. Sometimes, there is not enough value in the items to allow us to do that, so it could be that no money changes hands or we charge the seller for our clean-out service.

6. Traditional estate sale

A traditional estate sale involves pricing your items and setting them up at your site for a 2 or 3 day sale. If this method works best for you, we partner with a highly regarded and experienced estate sale firm. The fees for this service are similar to an online estate auction at your site.

7. Flea Market

You may choose to sell your items at our weekly Flea Market that takes place every Tuesday from April through September starting at 7 am at Mau’s Corner. There is no charge for your space. No sign-up is required, so if you would like to sell at the flea market, simply show up with your items.
