Items That Do Not Sell

These items generally do not sell well. That is not to say they don’t sell at all. Rather it means that there is little consignment value in them. If you see any of these at our auctions, it is probably because we have agreed to do an entire estate auction and sell everything for our client. If so, we will usually put many of these low value items in a group and sell them as a “box lot”. They will not be individually photographed or described. See the items that sell well.

Bad Consignment Items

  • Appliances, Kitchen
  • Appliances, Large
  • Appliances, Used
  • Blankets (unless special like a Hudson's Bay blanket)
  • Books such as Text Books, Reader's Digest, Encyclopedias and Books in Poor Condition. (Some coffee table books are still good)
  • Bowling Balls
  • Ceramic vases, bowls and figurines (unless very old)
  • Chemicals
  • Christmas Decorations (unless very old)
  • Christmas Trees (artificial)
  • Cleaning Supplies
  • Clocks (unless old)
  • Clothing (unless vintage, collectible, or high end)
  • Collector plates of all kinds
  • Computer Desks
  • Computers, Desktop
  • Consoles (such as old stero cabinets)
  • Cookie Jars (unless old or by a collectible maker)
  • Crutches and Walkers
  • Dinner and Drink Ware
  • Dolls, Unless Old and/or Porcelain
  • Electronics with missing parts or that do not work
  • Entertainment Centers
  • Exercise Equipment
  • Fax Machines
  • Fruit Jars, Clear
  • Furniture (damaged, particle board or upholstered)
  • Furniture, Damaged, Particle Board or Upholstered
  • Fur coats
  • Garage Sale Items
  • Glassware including pressed glass and clear glassware
  • Ice Skates
  • Ironing Boards
  • Lamps, Unless Unique or Antique
  • Luggage (unless high end or newer)
  • Magazines
  • Mattresses, Used
  • Meat grinders
  • Mirrors (unless unique)
  • National Geographic Magazines
  • Oil lamps (unless unique)
  • Paint
  • Pots and Pans
  • Printers
  • Records, Pre-Rock and Roll, Polkas, Lawrence Welk
  • Tins
  • Tires, Used
  • Towels
  • Treadmills
  • TVs (unless they are LCD, LED, or plasma)
  • Vacuums
  • VHS Tapes
  • Wall art (unless original or signed and numbered by a known artist)
